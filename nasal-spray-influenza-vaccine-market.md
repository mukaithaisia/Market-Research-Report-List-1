<p><p>The nasal spray influenza vaccine market is primarily divided into two types: inactive and live vaccines. Inactive nasal vaccines contain killed virus particles, prompting an immune response without causing illness. They are typically more stable and safe for specific populations, such as immunocompromised individuals. On the other hand, live nasal vaccines use weakened strains of the virus, stimulating a stronger immune response but may carry some risk for certain groups. Both types aim to enhance immunity and reduce flu transmission.</p></p>
