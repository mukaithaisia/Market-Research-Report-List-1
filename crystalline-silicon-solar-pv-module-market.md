<p><p>The crystalline silicon solar PV module market is primarily categorized into monocrystalline and polycrystalline types. Monocrystalline modules are known for high efficiency and space-saving design, while polycrystalline modules offer a lower-cost option with slightly reduced efficiency. Additionally, alternative technologies like cadmium telluride (CdTe) and copper indium gallium diselenide (CIGS) provide flexible and lightweight options suitable for various applications. Amorphous silicon (A-Si) is another thin-film technology, offering benefits such as lightweight and flexible installation, but generally has lower efficiency than crystalline counterparts.</p></p>
