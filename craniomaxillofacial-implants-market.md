<p><p>The craniomaxillofacial implants market encompasses two primary applications: external fixators and internal fixators. External fixators are devices applied externally to stabilize and support fractured bones or soft tissue injuries in the craniofacial region. In contrast, internal fixators are surgically implanted devices that provide internal support to facilitate proper healing of bone fractures or reconstructive procedures. Both types are essential in trauma cases, congenital deformities, and aesthetic surgeries, offering solutions for effective stabilization and improved patient outcomes in craniofacial procedures.</p></p>
